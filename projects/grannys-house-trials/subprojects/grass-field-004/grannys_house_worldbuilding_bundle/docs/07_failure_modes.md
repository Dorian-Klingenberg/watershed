# 07 — Failure-Mode Catalog

Failure should be instructive, visible, and usually recoverable.

## Surface overflow

Cause: Too much water released, blocked outlet, raised weir, clogged spillway.

Effect: Water overtops canal/terrace/channel and seeks a lower path.

Good use: Early visible chaos.

## Erosion cut

Cause: Fast water through soil or steep hard dirt.

Effect: Channel deepens or widens, creating unintended path.

Good use: Teaches velocity control and check dams.

## Seepage into basement

Cause: Standing water near north/west house wall, saturated hill, hidden conduit.

Effect: Basement dampness meter rises; eventually visible cellar flooding.

Good use: Signature house-protection risk.

## Boulder wall collapse

Cause: Climbing on loose rocks, removing support, fast flow undercutting base.

Effect: Rocks slump, block old route, or redirect water toward house.

Good use: Teaches stability and temporary solutions.

## Backwater pooling

Cause: Player blocks route without relief spillway.

Effect: Water level rises upstream until it escapes elsewhere.

Good use: Teaches that dams need outlets.

## Hidden reroute

Cause: Exposing old drain/conduit.

Effect: Water disappears and reappears somewhere bad, often basement or lower hazard zone.

Good use: Teaches testing unknown infrastructure.

## Garden drowning

Cause: Too much water or wrong terrace sequencing.

Effect: Lower beds flood, plants get damaged, soil muddies.

Good use: Shows primary goal can be overdone.

## Debris jam

Cause: Fast water carries sticks/stones/silt into gate, wheel, splitter, or drain.

Effect: Mechanism sticks open/closed or route blocks unexpectedly.

Good use: Gives root strainers and settling basins purpose.

## Domestic comedy failures

Examples:

- pumpkins float away
- path becomes mud
- Granny's preserves float in the basement
- flower beds become a pond
- tiny frogs colonize the wrong basin

These should reinforce systemic learning without making the player feel punished too harshly.
