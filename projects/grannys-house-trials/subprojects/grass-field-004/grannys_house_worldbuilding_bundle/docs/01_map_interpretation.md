# 01 — Map Interpretation

## Orientation

- North/top of the map is uphill.
- South/bottom of the map is downhill.
- Most water naturally wants to move from north to south.
- Hills and raised ground redirect water around objects, through channels, into pools, and toward hazards.

## Major features

### Infinite cistern

The cistern sits in the north/top-left area and acts as an infinite water source once the player opens or clears the intake channel. It is a tutorial simplification, but it creates the central danger: once released, water keeps coming.

Design role:

- source of water
- first ancient infrastructure object
- trigger for tutorial escalation
- source of over-success disasters

### Sturdy ancient canal

The sturdy canal is safe, reliable, high-capacity, and old. It can be altered, but it is not itself fragile. It is the "trusted" part of the system.

Design role:

- teaches that ancient infrastructure can be useful
- carries water safely toward the old garden
- provides a baseline contrast with loose dirt, loose rock, and improvised channels

### Loose boulder / stone wall

The boulder wall is unstable. It consists of loose stones or boulders, not engineered masonry. It can fail if climbed on, if support stones are removed, if water undercuts it, or if fast flow runs along it.

Design role:

- tempting shortcut boundary
- visible unstable material
- collapse/washout hazard
- teaches that some "walls" are not infrastructure

### Old terraced garden

The terraced garden is the first main target. Watering it should be relatively simple but still educational. It teaches elevation, overflow lips, terrace sequencing, partial blockages, and controlled release.

Design role:

- first success area
- low-stakes puzzle space
- teaches intended ancient water routing
- reveals that the mechanism still works after repair

### Granny's house

Granny's house sits downhill of the old garden. The basement is under the whole house. The north and west walls are built into a hill, making them especially vulnerable to seepage and water pressure.

Design role:

- beloved object to protect
- physical hydraulic obstacle
- domestic stakes
- basement failure/comedy state

### New lower garden

The new garden is downhill/south of the old garden and house. Reaching it sounds simple because water flows downhill, but the house, hill, boulder wall, and subsurface risks make it dangerous.

Design role:

- second-stage target
- introduces routing around protected structures
- requires more sophisticated flow control

## Basic level objective sequence

1. Repair or clear the ancient mechanism enough to open the cistern/channel.
2. Use the sturdy canal to water the old terraced garden.
3. Learn from terrace overflow, blockage, and flow control.
4. Attempt to route water downhill to the new garden.
5. Avoid flooding Granny's basement via surface flow, seepage, or hidden conduits.

## Signature trap

The obvious downhill route near the boulder wall appears to work briefly. Then fast flow undercuts or destabilizes loose rocks. The wall slumps or washes out, redirecting water toward Granny's house and basement.

This teaches: **a solution that works for thirty seconds is not necessarily stable.**
