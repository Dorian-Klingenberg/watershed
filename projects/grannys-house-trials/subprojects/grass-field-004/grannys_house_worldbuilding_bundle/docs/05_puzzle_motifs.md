# 05 — Puzzle Motifs

Puzzle motifs are reusable design patterns. Generated puzzles can combine two to four motifs.

## Motif: Blockage-as-control

Water source is too strong. Some weeds/debris are blocking flow. Removing all of the blockage causes overflow or erosion. Partial clearing is best.

Teaches:

- not all blockage is bad
- control can come from resistance
- "clean everything" is not always correct

## Motif: Loose-wall temptation

The shortest visible route follows or crosses the loose boulder wall. It works briefly but fails under high flow or disturbance.

Teaches:

- unstable materials can masquerade as boundaries
- temporary success is not stable success
- flow velocity and undercutting matter

## Motif: Hidden drain betrayal

A buried conduit appears to remove excess water. It may actually route water under the house, into a cellar drain, or away from the garden.

Teaches:

- old systems are coupled
- test unknown conduits with small flow first
- disappearance of water is not proof of success

## Motif: Backwater problem

The player blocks a dangerous route. Water then pools upstream and overtops somewhere worse.

Teaches:

- blocking flow stores energy/volume
- every dam needs a relief path
- water level is as important as direction

## Motif: Terrace sequencing

Upper terraces must fill before overflow feeds lower terraces. Wrong repairs skip levels or drown lower beds.

Teaches:

- elevation is logic
- overflow lips encode sequence
- ancient systems can be simple but precise

## Motif: Over-success surge

The player opens the cistern too much. The system works, but too well. It waters the garden, then drowns it, then overloads downstream routes.

Teaches:

- flow rate matters
- success metrics need safety constraints
- "more water" is not always better

## Motif: Useful danger

A contraption can solve one problem and create another.

Examples:

- check dam slows erosion but creates backwater
- weir activates safe side channel but raises level near foundation
- root strainer filters debris but blocks flow
- flume bypasses soil but leaks if overloaded
