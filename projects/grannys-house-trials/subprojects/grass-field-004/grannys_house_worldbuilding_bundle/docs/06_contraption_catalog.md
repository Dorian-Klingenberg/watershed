# 06 — Contraption & Infrastructure World-Building Catalog

Contraptions are not a replacement for the original matrix. They are world-building objects that later feed the interaction matrix and puzzle generator.

Design rule: **every contraption should be useful, dangerous, and legible.**

- Useful: it helps solve something.
- Dangerous: it can cause plausible failure.
- Legible: the player can eventually understand why it happened.

## Starter set for Granny's House Trials

### 1. Sluice gate

In-world description: A mossy stone-and-wood gate at the cistern outlet or canal intake.

Practical purpose: Starts, stops, and regulates water from the infinite cistern.

Player-readable clue: Water stains show safe normal flow height; gate handle/counterweight suggests adjustability.

Useful behavior: Lets player release gentle flow.

Dangerous behavior: Opening fully creates a surge before downstream system is ready.

Granny map placement: Cistern outlet, north/top-left.

Puzzle potential: First lesson in flow control.

### 2. Overflow lip / spillway

In-world description: A carved stone edge at a terrace boundary.

Practical purpose: Lets water fill one terrace before spilling into the next.

Player-readable clue: Wet mineral staining on the lip and old plant growth below the outlet.

Useful behavior: Creates controlled sequential watering.

Dangerous behavior: Missing/raised/blocked lip skips terraces or drowns lower beds.

Granny map placement: Old terraced garden.

Puzzle potential: Teaches "height is logic."

### 3. Check dam

In-world description: A small row of stones across a shallow channel.

Practical purpose: Slows water and reduces erosion on slopes.

Player-readable clue: Small upstream pool, calmer downstream flow.

Useful behavior: Makes steep dirt channels safer.

Dangerous behavior: Too many check dams cause backwater and overtopping.

Granny map placement: Route from old terraces toward new garden.

Puzzle potential: Lets player tune velocity.

### 4. Splitter box

In-world description: A small mossy stone basin with two or three carved exits.

Practical purpose: Divides canal flow between garden, overflow, bypass, or drain routes.

Player-readable clue: Multiple outlets at different heights, with old water marks.

Useful behavior: Player can choose/alter route by clearing/blocking outlets.

Dangerous behavior: Blocked outlets cause backwater or activate unsafe overflow.

Granny map placement: Between sturdy canal and terraced garden.

Puzzle potential: Visible routing choice.

### 5. Root strainer

In-world description: Living roots or old wooden/stone grates crossing a channel.

Practical purpose: Filters debris while slowing water.

Player-readable clue: Leaves/sticks trapped upstream; clear water downstream.

Useful behavior: Prevents debris from jamming mechanisms.

Dangerous behavior: Clearing roots can destabilize banks and increase flow too much.

Granny map placement: Canal entrance or dirt channel near garden.

Puzzle potential: Teaches weeds/roots may be useful.

### 6. Hidden drain / old conduit

In-world description: A buried stone-lined hole or half-sealed culvert.

Practical purpose: Unknown; part of older infrastructure.

Player-readable clue: Oddly shaped stone plug, damp patch, sound of water underground.

Useful behavior: Can bypass terrain or drain dangerous pools.

Dangerous behavior: May route water under Granny's house or into the basement.

Granny map placement: Near north/west house wall, hill, or old terrace overflow.

Puzzle potential: Signature "test before trusting" trap.

### 7. Weir

In-world description: A low barrier across a channel, often made from fitted stones.

Practical purpose: Raises upstream water level enough to enter side channels.

Player-readable clue: Calm raised water upstream, spill over a low edge.

Useful behavior: Activates a safe bypass or garden feeder.

Dangerous behavior: Raises water near foundation or overtops weak banks.

Granny map placement: Near route to lower garden.

Puzzle potential: Teaches water level control.

### 8. Rain barrel / gutter

In-world description: Granny's practical domestic water capture system.

Practical purpose: Collects roof runoff and redirects it away from foundation.

Player-readable clue: Gutters, downspouts, barrels, overflow hose.

Useful behavior: Provides controlled small water source or protects house from roof runoff.

Dangerous behavior: Clogged gutter dumps water against foundation; barrel overflows near basement.

Granny map placement: House exterior.

Puzzle potential: Makes the house mechanically relevant.

## Strong early combination

```text
cistern sluice
  → sturdy canal
  → splitter box
  → terraced overflow
  → weir/check dam
  → safe swale to new garden
```

Tempting disasters:

- hidden drain routes water under the house
- loose boulder wall collapses under fast flow
- direct downhill trench sends seepage to basement
