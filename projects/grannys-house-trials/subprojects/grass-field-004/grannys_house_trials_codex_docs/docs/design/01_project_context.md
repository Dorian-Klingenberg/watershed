# Project Context

## Working title

**Granny's House Trials**

## Role in larger game

This is the cozy tutorial/proving-ground area for a larger systems-first game about ancient, decaying water infrastructure. The player begins by helping Granny in the garden, but gradually discovers that the property is connected to old hydraulic machinery and forgotten infrastructure. Small, domestic water-routing tasks become the first controlled exposure to the larger game's core theme: partial understanding of coupled systems creates unintended consequences.

## Current map interpretation

The provided hand map and concept images describe a north-to-south downhill site.

Major features:

- North/top: cistern and hillside.
- Upper route: sturdy ancient canal.
- Upper/middle: old terraced garden.
- Middle/lower: Granny's house.
- House hazard: basement beneath the entire house.
- Special hazard: north and west house walls are built into the hill.
- West/side terrain: loose boulder wall / unstable rock pile.
- South/bottom: lower new garden.
- Terrain slopes generally north to south.
- Hills and ridges force water around objects, through choke points, or into pooling zones.

## Core gameplay loop for this prototype

1. Observe terrain and water features.
2. Modify terrain or infrastructure.
3. Release or redirect water.
4. Watch the system respond.
5. Compare outcome against goals.
6. Repair, mitigate, or exploit failures.

## Desired tone

Cozy surface, dangerous systems underneath.

The player should feel like they are doing a wholesome garden chore, but the mechanics should reveal engineering consequences. Failure should often be funny and recoverable: wet basement, floating pumpkins, muddy paths, washed-out rocks, annoyed but loving Granny.

## Non-goals for first prototype

Do not implement:

- A full 3D game.
- Real fluid dynamics.
- Full WFC.
- Complex AI characters.
- Polished art.
- Multiplayer.
- Save systems.
- Full erosion modeling.

The prototype should prove the puzzle-generation architecture, not the final presentation.
