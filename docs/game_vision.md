# Game Vision

## Working Vision
A fantasy exploration and intervention game set in a world whose ancient water-control civilization collapsed long ago, leaving aqueducts, mountain ice-domes, and climate-shaping infrastructure that still partly functions. Present-day societies live amid these remnants without truly understanding them.

The player moves through this world as someone who can observe, infer, and alter systems. Their actions can stabilize or destabilize local and regional conditions.

The ancient civilization should be understood as a society that solved enormous civilizational needs through passive infrastructure so successfully that it lost the pressure to renew the deepest knowledge behind that stability.

## Experience Goals
The game should create these feelings:

- curiosity about hidden structure
- respect for interconnected systems
- tension from incomplete knowledge
- satisfaction from successful diagnosis
- surprise from second-order effects
- unease about shortcut power
- the pleasure of realizing a previous mental model was incomplete

## The Present Built on the Past

The world is shaped not only by the remnants of an ancient, highly advanced civilization, but also by generations of modern improvisation layered on top of it.

The original builders created vast environmental systems:

- aqueduct networks
- water pressure systems
- climate-shaping structures
- regional flow controls

Over thousands of years, these systems have partially failed. The people who live in the present era rely on them but do not fully understand them.

To survive, communities have built their own **modifications, repairs, and grafted infrastructure** on top of the ancient systems.

Examples include:

- timber flumes attached to cracked stone canals
- hand-built spillways
- patched sluice gates
- sandbag flood barriers
- carved bypass trenches
- scavenged ancient components repurposed incorrectly
- ritual structures attached to misunderstood control mechanisms

These modern additions may stabilize or destabilize the system.

A repair that restores the original infrastructure may unintentionally destroy a fragile workaround that an entire community depends on.

The player operates inside this layered history of:

ancient design  
+ accumulated modification  
+ environmental consequence

## Player Fantasy
Not heroism.
Not domination.
Not loot accumulation.

The player fantasy is:

> becoming increasingly capable at reading and intervening in a living world built on forgotten infrastructure and misaligned powers.

Exploration should primarily reward knowledge, reinterpretation, and better models of cause, not only resources or progression tokens.

## Why This Game Exists
This game exists to let players explore complex systemic consequences through a fantasy world instead of through abstract dashboards alone.

It should make invisible dependencies tangible.
