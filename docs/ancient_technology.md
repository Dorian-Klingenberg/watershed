# Ancient Technology

## Canonical Model

Ancient infrastructure in this project is primarily fluidic, geometric, and passive.

Default assumption:

- no moving parts
- no hinges
- no pistons
- no ball valves
- no actuator logic

The ancients solved regulation problems by shaping water, pressure, trapped air, and terrain so the environment computed outcomes on its own.

## Environmental Computation

Ancient systems should be interpreted as environmental computation using water and terrain.

They compute by:

- accumulating water to thresholds
- sustaining or breaking siphons
- trapping or venting air
- delaying signals in basins
- biasing flow with geometry
- shedding excess water through spillways

## Core Mechanisms

### Gravity-Driven Flow

Elevation differences provide energy.
The ancients prefer height, slope, and basin placement over active forcing.

### Pressure Head

Water height acts as stored potential and as a signal.
High inland head can mean "release is ready" even though no mechanical device moves.

### Siphons

Siphons are the closest thing this setting has to a logic element.
They can:

- prime
- sustain
- break
- restart only when the correct pressure and air conditions return

### Air Traps

Air matters as much as water.
Chambers that retain or vent air determine whether siphon paths become stable or fail.

### Basins As Memory

A side basin can store water after a condition is met.
As long as it drains slowly enough, it preserves state after the original trigger weakens.

### Geometry As Control

The shape of the channel is the controller.
Narrow throats, lips, drop heights, branch angles, and asymmetrical routing all bias outcomes without moving mechanisms.

## Rare Moving Elements

Minimal moving remnants may exist, but they are rare and non-canonical.

If used, they should be:

- optional
- unnecessary for core operation
- degraded
- unreliable
- interpreted as later additions or edge-case exceptions

## Design Implication

Avoid presenting ancient ruins as machines with switches.
The player is not repairing a mechanism.
The player is intervening in a passive hydraulic computation that now computes incorrectly.
