# Game Design — Systems-First Infrastructure Simulation

## Core Concept

A world shaped by ancient hydraulic infrastructure that still partially functions.

The player:
- explores
- diagnoses
- repairs
- modifies

But:
> Local fixes create global consequences

---

## Design Philosophy

- Systems-first, not story-first
- Emergent behavior over scripted events
- Minimal exposition
- Discovery through experimentation

---

## Core Gameplay Loop

1. Observe system behavior
2. Form hypothesis
3. Intervene locally
4. Observe downstream consequences
5. Refine understanding

Progression = understanding

---

## Simulation Layers

Each tile contains interacting layers:

- Terrain
- Ancient Infrastructure
- Modern Modifications
- Water Flow
- Structural Condition
- Ecology
- Arcane Influence

---

## Key Mechanics

- Water routing (canals, aqueducts)
- Pressure-based behavior
- Threshold-triggered systems
- Delayed consequences
- System misbehavior due to degradation

---

## Failure as Gameplay

Systems do not simply break.

They:
- miscompute
- drift
- oscillate
- overcorrect

Examples:
- repairing a canal causes flooding downstream
- sealing a leak destroys a wetland
- restoring pressure destabilizes another region

---

## Magic Integration

Two paths:

Mastery:
- understanding systems
- stable outcomes

Spirit:
- borrowing power
- immediate results
- unpredictable consequences

Theme:
> Understanding vs shortcutting systems

---

## Core Identity

> A broken environmental control system, not a broken world
