# Roadmap

## Phase 1: Proof of Core Identity
Build a compact prototype where the player can inspect ancient water infrastructure, change one connection or state, advance simulation, and observe meaningful local + downstream results.

### Must-Haves
- compact map or region
- visible water / terrain stress state
- intervention interaction
- simulation tick or fast-forward
- consequence display
- minimal journal / map notes

### Current Vertical-Slice Candidate

The current strongest near-term project candidate is
[`Granny's House Trials`](/D:/Repos/Games/TheGame/projects/grannys-house-trials/README.md).

This is a small graphical testing project rather than the full game.
Its purpose is to prove that a tiny domestic space over hidden hydraulic
infrastructure can support:

- readable consequence
- repeatable testing rounds
- recurring tester personalities
- host-judged scoring based on tracked evidence

## Phase 2: Knowledge and Ambiguity
Add partial observability and interpretation tools.

### Examples
- uncertain readings
- incomplete records
- folk explanations vs real mechanisms
- better instruments through study

This phase should also reinforce archaeological ambiguity and the distinction between visible late-civilization meaning and hidden early-civilization function.

## Phase 3: Magic Layer
Introduce light and dark paths as alternate ways to perceive or influence the system.

## Phase 4: Social and Economic Consequences
Model settlement response, tradeoffs, adaptation, or migration.

## Phase 5: Content Expansion
Only after the above works, expand regions, factions, and story content.

## Long-Form Structure

The larger project should support:

- a complete Part 1 focused on lowlands, hydraulic adaptation, and knowledge growth
- a Part 2 focused on mountains, source-layer systems, and deeper causal correction
- a unified simulation that exists conceptually from the beginning

## Repeated Checkpoint
After every major feature, ask:

> Did this make the system more explorable, more legible, or more consequential?

If not, reconsider the feature.
