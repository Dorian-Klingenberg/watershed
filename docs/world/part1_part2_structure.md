# Part 1 And Part 2 Structure

## Core Principle

There is one world and one full simulation.
Part 1 and Part 2 are different access layers over the same reality.

The player does not enter a new system in Part 2.
The player learns to see more of the existing one.

## Part 1

### Focus

- lowlands and lower systems
- water flow
- pressure and head
- siphons
- thresholds
- local and regional hydraulic adaptation

### World Condition

Weather and seasonal flooding have been increasingly chaotic for centuries.
This instability is obvious throughout Part 1, but nobody understands the true cause.

### Player Role

The player:

- stabilizes local systems
- builds resilience
- makes sensible local repairs
- learns water logic
- gains knowledge through exploration

Part 1 must stand on its own and feel complete.

## Part 2

### Focus

- high mountains
- geothermal systems
- deep source-layer infrastructure
- atmospheric coupling
- dwarf or deep tunnel layers
- deeper ancient technology

### Role

Part 2 reveals that Part 1 instability was a symptom, not the whole problem.
The player begins correcting deeper causes rather than only adapting locally.

## Hidden Full Simulation

The world always operates on the full system.
Part 1 does not fully expose that system.

This means:

- deeper thermal and atmospheric layers may already be conceptually active
- Part 1 problems must still be locally solvable
- Part 2 recontextualizes rather than invalidates Part 1

## Commercial And Structural Goal

Part 1 should create a compelling urge to continue into Part 2, but not through cheap cliffhangers.

The better outcome is:

- Part 1 is satisfying
- the player solves real problems
- their mental model becomes stronger
- by the end they realize the model is still incomplete

This should feel like layered discovery, not a mystery box withholding fake answers.

## Reconciliation Pass

An optional Part 2 endgame can allow the player to revisit Part 1 regions and refine earlier fixes under deeper understanding.

This works best when:

- Part 1 fixes were reasonable under partial knowledge
- Part 2 exposes deeper causes
- the same unified simulation supports better second-pass interventions
