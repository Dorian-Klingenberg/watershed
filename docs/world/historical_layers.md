# Historical Layers

## Purpose

The world should distinguish clearly between early or peak civilization layers and late civilization layers.

This is not a minor aesthetic distinction.
It is a core interpretive structure for the setting.

## Early / Peak Civilization

Characteristics:

- functional
- precise
- minimal ornament
- engineering-first
- infrastructure-focused
- physically essential
- often hard to access
- culturally invisible in the present

These are the source-layer works that actually make the world function.
They are often found in mountains, deep conduits, high terraces, buried channels, and remote control sites.

## Late Civilization

Characteristics:

- grand
- ornate
- symbolic
- reverential
- cathedral-like
- often built around or atop older systems
- only partially understanding the deeper infrastructure

Late civilization architecture tends to make the system visible, meaningful, and sacred without necessarily preserving full technical comprehension.

## Core Theme

As understanding declined, expression and reverence replaced comprehension.

That means:

- early works are physically necessary but culturally obscure
- late works are culturally dominant but technically secondary

## Modern Relationship

Modern people inherit much more from the late layer than the early one.

They inherit from the late layer:

- architecture
- symbols
- myths
- rituals
- sacred spaces
- visible memory

But they remain physically dependent on the early layer:

- hidden infrastructure
- source control systems
- fluidic logic
- transport networks
- environmental regulation

The visible layer is trusted.
The invisible layer is what actually matters.
