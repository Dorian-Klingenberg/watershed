# Knowledge Geography

## Core Principle

Knowledge is distributed geographically.
Moving through the world should feel like moving through levels of truth.

## High Mountains: Source Layer

The high mountains contain:

- the oldest infrastructure
- source control systems
- glacial management
- high-head reservoirs
- later geothermal and atmospheric coupling sites

These places are:

- remote
- dangerous
- difficult to reach
- sparsely inhabited
- foundational to the whole system

They are where the deepest truths of the world still physically reside.

## Lowlands: Inherited Layer

The lowlands contain:

- reused infrastructure
- visible ruins
- ritualized systems
- modified transport works
- settlements living inside inherited hydraulic logic

These are the systems people actually know.
They are not false, but they are incomplete.

## Knowledge Loss

Knowledge was lost partly because later peoples no longer reached the true source-layer ruins.

Once the mountain control layer became inaccessible:

- maintenance degraded
- understanding narrowed
- myths replaced direct knowledge
- late symbolic interpretation expanded

The most important systems did not vanish.
They became too far away to remain ordinary.

## Progression Implication

Lowland exploration should teach practical water logic.
Mountain exploration should recontextualize the meaning of the whole world.
