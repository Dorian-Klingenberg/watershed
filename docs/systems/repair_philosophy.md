# Repair Philosophy

## Core Principle

The world does not need to be rebuilt.
It needs to be understood and gently corrected.

Ancient systems are often still mostly functional.
They usually do not need total reconstruction.

They can often be restored or stabilized through:

- patching obvious breakages
- clearing silt in the correct locations
- reopening blocked spillways
- restoring air pockets
- correcting small geometry problems

## Why This Matters

Modern people usually do not understand that small interventions are often enough.
They commonly:

- over-fix
- mis-fix
- optimize the wrong thing
- remove features that were actually part of the current balance

Maintenance without understanding is often destructive.

## Gameplay Implication

The player should learn that:

- some damage is real damage
- some apparent damage is a stabilizing adaptation
- some "waste" paths are actually safety paths
- gentle correction can be more powerful than total rebuild

## Part 1 And Part 2

Part 1 solutions are valid local adaptations under partial understanding.
Part 2 allows deeper reinterpretation of those same interventions.

The later game should not humiliate earlier play.
It should reveal that earlier repairs were intelligent but incomplete.
