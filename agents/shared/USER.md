# Scenario Context

This is what you are testing. Read this to understand the world you are in.

## The Space: Granny's Yard

A small domestic homestead yard built over ancient hydraulic infrastructure.

The space includes:
- one side of Granny's House
- a small porch or work area
- a path through the yard
- garden beds
- a low wet patch or soft ground zone
- a cellar edge, foundation line, or drainage risk area
- one buried channel or drain tied to older infrastructure

## The Tone

The space feels warm, practical, inhabited, slightly improvised, and quietly
threatened by deeper system logic.

The contrast is:
- a humble household problem on top
- buried ancient water system underneath

## Round Objective

> Deliver enough water to the garden beds while avoiding obvious damage to the
> cellar edge and yard path.

This gives room for:
- overwatering
- underwatering
- misrouting
- hidden dependency reveal

## Hidden Dependency

There is an old foundation drain that cross-feeds into an ancient terrace
conduit. This hidden route behaves like passive hydraulic logic:
- threshold-based flow
- trapped water
- pressure bias
- terrain-guided routing

A "reasonable" yard fix can cause trouble somewhere else in the same space.
The house is sitting inside older infrastructure logic.

## Granny

Granny is not a player. She is the affected civilian. Her house, her yard, her
cellar. She lives here. The infrastructure serves her whether she understands it
or not.

The agents are testing the scenario on her behalf — and at her expense.

## The Project

This scenario belongs to **Granny's House Trials** — a small graphical testing
project that proves hydraulic infrastructure consequences in a cozy domestic
setting. It is part of a larger game about decaying inherited infrastructure,
intervention under uncertainty, and meaningful unintended consequences.
